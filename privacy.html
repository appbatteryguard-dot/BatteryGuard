2. Scope of this policy

This policy applies to the BatteryGuard Android application, the BatteryGuard public website, email support interactions, purchase validation for paid features, smart-plug integrations enabled by the user, and the product recommendation / affiliate-link section described on the current landing page.

BatteryGuard is offered globally, but this policy is intentionally drafted to meet a GDPR / EU level of transparency. Where mandatory local law gives you stronger rights, those rights continue to apply.

BatteryGuard’s current landing page states that the app provides battery monitoring, loud alarms, charging schedules, charger comparison, smart-plug cutoff, Pro purchases, and affiliate-linked accessory recommendations. This policy is written to match those live representations.

3. Data we process

3.1 Data processed locally on your device

To provide BatteryGuard’s core features, the app may process technical and device-level information such as:

  • battery level, charging state, battery temperature, charging power estimates, and charge-session timing;
  • charging history stored by the app, such as daily or limited historical measurements shown inside the app;
  • user-defined settings, such as charge limit, alarm behavior, selected language, widget preferences, schedules, and smart-plug configuration choices;
  • state information needed for foreground-service operation, reboot restart handling, alarms, notifications, or widget refreshes.

Wherever technically possible, this information is processed and stored locally on your device rather than transmitted to BatteryGuard’s operator.

3.2 Smart-plug and local integration data

If you enable automatic cutoff or compatible-plug features, BatteryGuard may process technical information needed to connect to, identify, and control supported devices, including local network details, local identifiers, Bluetooth pairing details, device names, model information, or vendor integration settings required to make the feature work.

3.3 Purchase and entitlement data

If you purchase BatteryGuard Pro, BatteryGuard may process the minimum data needed to verify and maintain your purchase entitlement, such as store transaction status, entitlement state, or non-financial purchase verification data made available by the relevant app store or platform.

3.4 Support and contact data

If you contact BatteryGuard by email, we process the email address you use, your message, and any information you decide to include in your support request.

3.5 Website access data

When you visit the public BatteryGuard website, the hosting or delivery providers may necessarily process standard technical request information such as IP address, browser or device metadata, timestamps, and requested resources in order to serve the website and keep it secure.

3.6 Affiliate and external-shop interactions

The BatteryGuard website or app may display external product links, including affiliate links. If you click those links, the external seller, marketplace, or affiliate platform may process information about the click or purchase under its own privacy policy.

3.7 Data BatteryGuard does not intentionally collect for its core functionality

  • contacts, call logs, SMS content, photos, videos, or microphone recordings;
  • precise location, unless a future feature clearly requires it and this policy is updated first;
  • government ID numbers, payment card details, or banking credentials;
  • special-category personal data unless you choose to send such information to us in a support message.
Please do not send unnecessary personal or sensitive data by email. In particular, do not send government ID numbers, card data, passwords, or health information unless it is strictly necessary and legally justified.

4. Why we use the data

  • to monitor charging behavior and show live battery information;
  • to trigger alarms when the user-selected charge limit is reached;
  • to operate charging schedules, widgets, notifications, and foreground-service behavior;
  • to connect to and control supported smart plugs chosen by the user;
  • to validate Pro access and keep paid features available after purchase;
  • to answer support requests, solve technical issues, and prevent misuse;
  • to maintain the website and measure or defend against abuse, fraud, or security incidents;
  • to comply with legal obligations and defend legal claims when necessary.

5. Legal bases for processing

Where GDPR or similar law applies, BatteryGuard relies on one or more of the following legal bases depending on the processing activity:

  • Performance of a contract or pre-contractual steps: for the app’s core functions, Pro entitlement verification, and user-requested support.
  • Consent: where optional permissions, optional integrations, or voluntary communications make consent the appropriate basis.
  • Legitimate interests: to secure the service, prevent abuse, document incidents, answer non-account support, and maintain a working public website, provided those interests are not overridden by your rights.
  • Legal obligation: where records must be kept or data must be disclosed under applicable law.
If a future BatteryGuard feature accesses personal or sensitive data in a way that is not obvious from the user experience, Google Play may require an in-app prominent disclosure and, where required, consent before the feature starts collecting or accessing that data.

6. Sharing and recipients

BatteryGuard does not sell personal data. Data may nevertheless be shared or made available to the following categories of recipients when necessary:

  • app-store and payment ecosystem providers, such as Google Play, for app distribution, purchase validation, and compliance;
  • hosting and infrastructure providers, such as website-hosting or delivery services needed to publish the landing page and legal documents;
  • smart-plug or integration providers selected by the user, where connectivity to their ecosystem is required;
  • email and support service providers, only to the extent necessary to receive and respond to communications;
  • professional advisers, authorities, or courts, when disclosure is required by law or reasonably necessary to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims.
Third-party products
BatteryGuard’s landing page currently refers to Tapo, Shelly, BLE smart plugs, Amazon, AliExpress, and Mercado Libre. Those third parties have their own privacy and contractual terms.
Affiliate links
If you click an affiliate link, tracking of that referral may take place under the external platform’s policy so that a commission can be attributed.

7. International data transfers

Because BatteryGuard is distributed globally and may rely on international providers, some data may be processed outside the European Economic Area. Where such transfers take place and GDPR applies, BatteryGuard will seek to rely on a lawful transfer mechanism, such as an adequacy decision, standard contractual clauses, or another safeguard recognized by applicable law.

External retailers, marketplaces, smart-plug vendors, and app-distribution platforms may act under their own international structures and privacy policies.

8. Retention and deletion policy

Data category Typical retention approach
Local battery history, limits, schedules, and app preferences Stored on the device until you delete the app, clear app storage, reset the feature, or overwrite the data.
Smart-plug pairing or configuration data stored locally by the app Retained until you remove the integration, clear app data, delete the app, or the data is no longer needed for the feature.
Support emails and related correspondence Kept for the time needed to resolve the matter and normally for up to 24 months afterwards, unless a longer period is necessary for legal claims or legal compliance.
Purchase entitlement or compliance records Retained for as long as needed to prove your entitlement, prevent fraud, and comply with tax, accounting, or legal obligations where applicable.
Website technical logs held by hosting or delivery providers Retained according to the relevant provider’s operational and security policy, unless BatteryGuard controls a shorter deletion cycle.

When deletion is requested, BatteryGuard will delete or anonymize data that it controls unless retention is still necessary for legal claims, fraud prevention, security, or a legal obligation.

9. Security measures

BatteryGuard applies a data-minimization approach and aims to keep as much processing local as reasonably possible. Additional safeguards may include:

  • restricting access to support or legal correspondence to authorized persons only;
  • using reputable platforms for app distribution, payments, hosting, and email where appropriate;
  • reviewing the amount of personal data collected so that the app does not process more than is reasonably needed;
  • taking technical and organizational measures proportionate to the risk of the processing.

No service can guarantee absolute security. Users should also keep their devices updated and avoid sending excessive personal data through support channels.

10. Your privacy rights

If GDPR or similar law applies to you, you may have the right to be informed, access your data, rectify inaccurate data, request erasure, restrict processing, object to certain processing, request portability where applicable, and not be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing in the circumstances provided by law.

To exercise your rights, contact support@batteryguard.me and clearly describe your request. We may ask for reasonable proof of identity where necessary to protect your data from unauthorized disclosure.

Response timing
Under GDPR practice, requests should generally be answered within one month, although this may be extended in more complex cases where the law allows it.
Complaints
If you are in Spain, you may lodge a complaint with the Agencia Española de Protección de Datos (AEPD). If you are elsewhere, you may contact your local supervisory authority.

11. Children’s privacy

BatteryGuard is not directed to children as a primary audience. If local law requires parental authorization for digital services or data processing, the app should only be used in compliance with that law.

If you believe a child has provided personal data to BatteryGuard in breach of applicable law, please contact us so we can review the situation.

12. Changes to this policy

BatteryGuard may update this Privacy Policy to reflect changes in the app, website, integrations, legal obligations, or business model. The latest version will be posted on this page with the updated date shown above.

If future versions of BatteryGuard add new personal-data uses, new permissions, or new third-party SDKs, this policy should be updated before those changes go live.
