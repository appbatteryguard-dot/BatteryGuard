The problem

Your battery is dying.
Every night.

Most people charge their phone overnight. That means hours at 100% — the worst state for a lithium battery.

🔋

100% kills batteries

Keeping a lithium battery at 100% for hours accelerates degradation. Ideal range is 20–80%.

🌡️

Heat while charging

Fast chargers generate heat. A noname charger can push 42°C+ — every degree counts against longevity.

🔔

Alarms you sleep through

AccuBattery's alarm lasts 1 second. Easy to miss at 3am. Your phone keeps charging until morning.

FAQ

Good questions.

Do I need a smart plug to use BatteryGuard? +
No. The free version works without any hardware — it sends a loud, full-screen alarm when your battery hits the limit so you can disconnect manually. The smart plug unlocks automatic disconnection (Pro feature).
Which smart plugs are compatible? +
Currently: TP-Link Tapo P110/P115 (WiFi), Shelly Plug S / Plus Plug S (WiFi + BLE), and generic BLE smart plugs (Bluetooth 5.0). More brands coming in future updates.
Does it work while the phone is asleep? +
Yes. BatteryGuard runs as a foreground service — it stays active in the background and restarts automatically after a reboot.
Is the Pro purchase really one-time? +
Yes. €4.99 once, and the app is yours forever. Updates are included for 1 year. After that, if we release meaningful new features, you can choose to renew at a reduced price — but if nothing changes, nothing is charged.
Does it drain the battery itself? +
BatteryGuard uses the system's BatteryManager broadcast — it doesn't poll constantly. The foreground service has minimal CPU and memory footprint, typically under 1% battery impact per day.
What about the affiliate links in the shop? +
We only recommend products we've tested and verified as compatible. If you buy through our links, we earn a small commission at no extra cost to you. This helps us keep the free version truly free.
